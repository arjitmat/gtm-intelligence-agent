# Battlecard — Vena

> Last updated: 2026-04-22
> Owner: Competitive Intel Lead
> Status: ACTIVE — encountered when buyer is heavily Excel-attached

## Snapshot

- **HQ:** Toronto, Canada
- **Founded:** 2011
- **Employees:** ~720
- **Funding:** $366M total, $75M growth round Nov 2024 (Vista Equity + JMI Equity)
- **Where they win:** Excel-native finance teams, North American mid-market, Microsoft-aligned shops
- **Where we win:** Buyers ready to move past Excel limits, EU mid-market, multi-dimensional models

---

## Positioning (How They Pitch)

"Excel-powered FP&A." Their pitch is uniquely defensive: *"don't change your finance team's habits — give them a better engine underneath."* Tight Microsoft 365 / Power BI alignment. They explicitly position against "rip-and-replace" tools.

This is a coherent and durable position. Don't dismiss it.

---

## Strengths

1. **Excel-native.** Zero retraining for finance teams. Adoption near 100% from day 1.
2. **Microsoft 365 + Power BI integration.** Tight, mature, frictionless for Microsoft-aligned organisations.
3. **North American mid-market presence.** Strong references in services, manufacturing, healthcare.
4. **Multi-currency / multi-entity adequate.** Handles mid-market consolidation cases well.
5. **Procurement-friendly.** Often already on Microsoft frameworks → faster legal.

---

## Weaknesses (Where We Win)

1. **Excel-as-engine has a ceiling.** When models grow past ~20 dimensions or 10M cells, performance degrades. Power users hit this within 18 months.
2. **Limited collaboration features.** Excel + Vena ≠ real multi-user planning. Version control still spreadsheet-style.
3. **EU presence small.** Limited support hours, no native EU data residency.
4. **Clunky scenario comparison.** Side-by-side scenario UX dated.
5. **Brand drift.** Vista ownership signals cost discipline > innovation.

---

## Typical Objections & Responses

**Objection: "My team lives in Excel — Vena means no retraining."**
Response: Honest framing — that's a real cost we acknowledge. The question is: are you buying a tool for today's team or for the team you'll have after Series C? Our customers report 2–3 weeks of retraining offset by 6–8 months of capability gains. Happy to share the studies.

**Objection: "Vena's Power BI integration is critical for us."**
Response: We integrate with Power BI too — happy to show it. The deeper question: if BI is your visualisation layer, are you optimising for the modelling layer or for Excel comfort? Different decisions follow.

**Objection: "We'll switch off Vena when we outgrow it."**
Response: Migration off Vena is harder than people expect — Excel-encoded business logic is the hardest thing to port. The cost of staying on Excel-as-engine compounds over years. Want to see the audit we run with prospective customers?
