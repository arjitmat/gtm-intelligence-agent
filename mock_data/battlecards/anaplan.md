# Battlecard — Anaplan

> Last updated: 2026-04-22
> Owner: Competitive Intel Lead
> Status: ACTIVE — encountered mostly in displacement deals (incumbent → us)

## Snapshot

- **HQ:** Miami, FL (post-Thoma Bravo take-private, 2022)
- **Founded:** 2006
- **Employees:** ~2,400
- **Ownership:** Private — Thoma Bravo, $10.7B take-private (June 2022)
- **Where they win:** Fortune 1000, multi-entity, finance + supply chain + workforce planning together
- **Where we win:** Mid-market displacement, faster TTV, dramatically lower TCO

---

## Positioning (How They Pitch)

"Connected planning for the enterprise." Their narrative is breadth: finance, sales, supply chain, workforce — all in one platform on the Hyperblock engine. They lead with industry templates and customer logos (Coca-Cola, P&G, HP, Linde).

Post Thoma-Bravo their messaging has tilted toward operational efficiency and industry-vertical solutions, away from product-velocity claims.

---

## Strengths

1. **Hyperblock calculation engine.** Genuinely powerful for huge models — handles billions of cells without breaking.
2. **Enterprise install base.** Network effects in F1000: "everyone uses it" is a real argument at that tier.
3. **Multi-domain (finance + supply chain + workforce).** Buyers wanting one platform across functions still pick Anaplan.
4. **Industry accelerator templates.** Pharma, CPG, retail — pre-built models save months of design.
5. **Trust at the CFO level.** Boring is a feature when you're a $5B revenue company.

---

## Weaknesses (Where We Win — Especially in Displacement)

1. **Implementation 4–9 months.** Partner-led (Deloitte, Accenture, Wipro). Implementation often costs more than software. **We're 3–5 weeks, no partner required.**
2. **License cost.** Entry typically €150k+/yr; full enterprise deals routinely €500k–2M ARR. **We compress that 3–5x.**
3. **Innovation velocity post-Thoma Bravo.** Reduced eng headcount, slower release cadence. Product reviews increasingly cite stagnation.
4. **UX dated.** Modellers love it; non-finance users find it impenetrable. Adoption beyond finance team often fails.
5. **Modeller dependency.** Each company needs 1–3 dedicated "Anaplan modellers" — a real cost line, not just a soft cost.
6. **Locked-in renewal pricing.** Customers report 8–15% annual escalators with no easy off-ramp.

## Typical Objections & Responses

**Objection: "We need enterprise scale — only Anaplan does this."**
Response: True for Fortune 100. For mid-market, you don't have a Hyperblock-scale problem — you have a faster-close problem and a better-collaboration problem. Let's test that with your actual model size.

**Objection: "We've already invested €X in Anaplan."**
Response: Sunk cost. The right question is the next 3 years' TCO. We routinely show 60–70% reduction over 3 years for mid-market customers, including switching cost. Want a TCO model?

**Objection: "Migration risk is too high."**
Response: We have a migration accelerator: parallel run for 6 weeks, your team validates outputs side-by-side. We've done this 11 times in the last 18 months — happy to share two reference customers.

**Objection: "Anaplan has industry templates we'd lose."**
Response: For [their industry], we can map their existing model to ours in [X days]. The template is the starting point, not the finished model — and your finished model migrates 1:1.
