# Battlecard — Planful

> Last updated: 2026-04-22
> Owner: Competitive Intel Lead
> Status: ACTIVE — most common in US mid-market deals; declining in EU pipeline

## Snapshot

- **HQ:** Redwood City, CA, USA
- **Founded:** 2001 (originally Host Analytics, rebranded 2020)
- **Employees:** ~530
- **Ownership:** Vector Capital (acquired Aug 2021)
- **Where they win:** US mid-market, services + financial services verticals, close + consolidation–heavy buyers
- **Where we win:** EU mid-market, scenario-modelling–led evaluations, faster product velocity

---

## Positioning (How They Pitch)

"The financial performance platform for the mid-market." They emphasise close + consolidation depth (legacy of Host Analytics), then layer planning and reporting. Buyer narrative: "trusted, mid-market-native, not enterprise-overkill."

Recent (2026) directional shift: vertical plays into PE portfolio companies and healthcare services. Watch their blog — vertical content is the leading indicator.

---

## Strengths

1. **Close + consolidation depth.** Multi-entity, multi-currency, intercompany eliminations — strong from the Host Analytics era.
2. **Mid-market US brand.** Recognised name with finance recruiters in the US — easier internal sell.
3. **Vector Capital backing.** Stable ownership, no near-term financial pressure.
4. **Reporting flexibility.** Drag-and-drop reports favoured by accounting-heavy teams.

---

## Weaknesses (Where We Win)

1. **Slower product velocity.** Release cadence noticeably below Pigment and us. UX shows it — feels 2018.
2. **Weaker scenario modelling.** Reports of clunky version comparison and limited driver-based modelling.
3. **EU presence thin.** No native EU data residency offering. Limited EU-based customer success staff.
4. **Acquisition fatigue.** Being a Vector Capital portfolio asset means cost discipline > innovation. Customers report this.
5. **Implementation 8–14 weeks.** Comparable to Pigment, slower than us.

---

## Typical Objections & Responses

**Objection: "Planful is the safe mid-market choice."**
Response: Safe in the US. In Europe, we have native data residency, EU-based support, and EU customer references. Different market, different "safe."

**Objection: "Their close + consolidation is best in class."**
Response: For accounting-led teams, true. For FP&A-led teams who do planning and reporting AND close, we cover all three; Planful is strong on close, weak on planning. What's your team's primary daily job?

**Objection: "We already use them at our parent company."**
Response: Worth understanding the contract structure. Often subsidiary-level deals are independent — and EU subs frequently outgrow Planful's EU support. Happy to map this.
